Does it work?

Fair question. Does any of this actually work?

We tend to ignore what we can’t measure. So here’s something you can measure: my resting heart rate, dropping from 70 to 51 over a year of freediving. Calmer body, calmer mind — the body keeping score while the inner work went on.

The heart rate is just the easiest number to show. Everything else I checked the way a miner checks: does the reward keep coming? Proof of work is just another word for proof it works. The mood I live in these days was mined out of ordinary practice — waking at the same hour, meditation, the sea. And the test runs itself: do the work and it arrives; skip a few days and it stops. Stockpiling fails too — joy expires at midnight.

The strangest result is luck. I catch myself saying it out loud: things go my way, constantly. Everything starts happening the moment I so much as stir — events land so densely they pile onto each other, and I barely finish one before the next begins. Friends across the world invite me to stay. Even the bad news keeps turning out, months later, to be the best thing that could have happened. In a contented universe there are no failures; somehow everything works out. And I work because I like the work — money gets sprinkled on top, never so much that it gets in the way of training.

I can’t prove it for you. The proof is something you have to run on yourself. The best I can do is pass it on.

So run it. This experiment has exactly one lab — your life. There are others like it. But this one is yours.
