Resident: Two Years of Oakenfold at Cream

Cream started in October 1992 as a weekly house night at Nation, a club in Wolstenholme Square, Liverpool. By the mid-90s it was pulling around 3,000 people a week, and Paul Oakenfold held a residency there from 1997 to 1999. “Resident” collects two years of those sets into a double CD, released on Virgin on 15 March 1999.

The two discs run just over two and a half hours total, moving through progressive house and trance with a few detours into breakbeat and acid house. Names on the tracklist include Tilt, Underworld, Armin van Buuren, Push, and CJ Bolland — mostly UK and European producers who defined the sound of British superclubs in the late 90s.

AllMusic’s Jason Birchmeier called it “a picture-perfect snapshot of a memorable moment in dance-music history,” and gave it four stars. The Nation building itself was demolished in 2016; Cream’s weekly night at that address ran until 2002 before the brand moved on to Creamfields and residencies elsewhere.
